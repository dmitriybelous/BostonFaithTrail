# Tremont Temple Baptist Church

## Overview

Tremont Temple Baptist Church has been a landmark on Tremont Street since the 19th century and is known for its abolition-era history. The congregation became famous for offering free seating, which made worship accessible to people regardless of social class.

## Historical Significance

The church became an important civic and religious platform in Boston, hosting major speakers and reform events. In the years before the Civil War, anti-slavery voices gathered here, making Tremont Temple part of the city’s public struggle over freedom and justice.

## Architecture and Setting

The current building, completed in the late 1800s, was designed as both a church and public venue. Its location places it within easy walking distance of Park Street, King’s Chapel, and Old South Meeting House, creating a dense corridor of religious and civic history.
