# Boston Irish Famine Memorial

## Overview

The Boston Irish Famine Memorial is a sculpture and plaza installation between School Street and Washington Street. It commemorates the Great Famine in Ireland and the migration of Irish families who helped shape Boston’s neighborhoods, labor history, and public life.

## Meaning

The paired sculptural groups contrast hunger and displacement with survival and opportunity in America. This visual transition is central to the memorial’s message: remembrance of suffering and recognition of immigrant resilience.

## Trail Context

Located across from Old South Meeting House, this stop sits at a crossroads of revolutionary memory, immigrant history, and public commemoration. It is one of the most accessible and frequently visited reflection points in the downtown trail area.
